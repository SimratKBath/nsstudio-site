Why this exists.

NS Studio LLC is a California-based advisory studio working with EdTech founders, mid-market operators, and seed-stage teams who need to make AI decisions that actually pay back.

Why this studio exists

The AI hype cycle has produced more decks than products. Most companies we've spoken with have a similar story: they ran one or two AI pilots, hired or didn't hire, tried two or three tools, and at the end of a year couldn't tell you which decisions paid back. Not because the people were bad, they weren't, but because nobody was paid to make hard calls.

NS Studio exists for that gap. We are paid specifically to make the hard calls, to tell a founder which AI product is worth building, which feature should die, which workflow can be redesigned this quarter, and which vendor relationship is quietly costing more than it returns.

The frameworks we use are adapted from the way Paul Graham evaluates startups, pressure-testing core assumptions, finding fatal flaws early, and ranking opportunities by genuine differentiation rather than buzz. We've adapted those frameworks for AI product decisions inside companies, where the stakes are quieter but the cost of being wrong is the same: time you can't get back.

The way we work

Founder-led. Every engagement is run by a senior partner. We don't field junior consultants on client work. The whole point of a boutique is concentrated attention , if you're not getting it, you're paying for the wrong thing.

Framework-driven. We don't reinvent the engagement shape per client. The same diagnostic, the same ranking exercise, the same explicit kill list, applied to your specific business. Predictability is a feature.

Selective. We work with a small number of clients at any one time, by referral or after a fit call. The reason is simple: senior attention is the product. The moment that gets diluted, the work gets worse.

Honest. We tell you when we're not the right fit. We tell you when an engagement won't pay back. We tell you when the answer is to do less, not more. The currency of an advisor is honesty, once it's gone, the rest doesn't matter.

Operating principles

  1. The most valuable thing we say is sometimes "don't build that."
  2. If a deliverable could have been written by another consultancy, we should have used that consultancy.
  3. Capacity is a feature. Senior attention is what you're paying for, so we keep the practice small.
  4. Ship the decision, not the deck.
  5. If the engagement isn't paying for itself by week two, end it cleanly.
