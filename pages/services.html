Honesty matters

What we deliberately don't do.

The fastest way to know if a partner is right is to know what they refuse. Here's our list.

  • We don't sell decks. Every deliverable is a working decision document, plan, or specification — not a slide-ware showpiece.
  • We don't take on engagements where we'd be the sixth opinion. If you've already had three consultancies in and decisions still aren't being made, the problem isn't strategy — it's something else, and we'll tell you what.
  • We don't build production code. We design the spec, pick the tools, and supervise the build — but we partner with engineering teams (yours or ours), not become them.
  • We don't take more than three engagements per quarter. The whole point is senior, founder-led attention. Capacity is the feature.
How we think

The same diagnostic. Every engagement.

We don't reinvent the framework for each client. We adapt one. The version below is the prompt we run on every engagement — the question we are paid to answer. 

<role>
Act as an AI product evaluator who has
reviewed hundreds of pilots and knows
which ones pay back and which ones
quietly die.
</role>

<task>
Pressure-test the AI roadmap. Find every
fatal flaw before another quarter is
wasted on the wrong thing.
</task>

<rules>
- Every flaw specific to this business
- Verdict direct — never "it has
  potential but"
- Fatal flaws ranked by severity
</rules>

<output>
Verdict → Three Fatal Flaws →
Highest-Profit Product → 90-Day Plan
</output>

Why a prompt, not a deck.

A deck describes a method. A prompt forces it. We write the diagnostic in this form because it survives delegation — you can hand it to anyone senior in your org, run it on your roadmap, and get a useful answer in an afternoon.

The full version we run includes 14 sub-prompts, calibration questions, and the priors we use to weigh switching costs against ROI. Clients receive it as part of the Strategy Sprint deliverable.
